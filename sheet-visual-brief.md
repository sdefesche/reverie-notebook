# Reverie — Traveler Sheet
## Visual Design Brief for a Physical Sheet

**For:** a graphic designer or image-generation agent producing the printed character sheet.
**Not in scope:** HTML, interactivity, code of any kind. This is art direction for a paper object.

---

## 1. The object and its life

This sheet will be printed at home or at a copy shop, folded into a coat pocket, filled in with a pencil on a train, taped or glued into a shared notebook, rained on, and photographed. Design for that life. It is not a product shot; it is a companion. A sheet that looks *too finished* will intimidate the pencil. The design succeeds when a player feels invited — almost obligated — to draw on it.

**Design thesis, one line:** *a page from a wanderer's field journal, printed by a small-town press that also prints train tickets.*

## 2. Mood

**Words to design toward:** liminal · attentive · well-worn · quiet · slightly uncanny · ticket-office · fog at dusk · pressed flowers · misdelivered mail.

**Words to design away from:** epic · heroic · medieval · parchment-scroll · dungeon · ornate · gothic · grimdark · whimsical-cute · corporate-clean.

Reverie is a travel game about noticing, riddles, and things found on real journeys. Its fantasy is the strangeness of ordinary places — a lit window in an empty building — never dragons. If any element would look at home on a D&D sheet, remove it.

## 3. Format & print

- **Primary format: A5 portrait**, single-sided. Everything fits one page.
- **Variants:** A4 with two A5 sheets side by side (cut line marked with a tiny scissors glyph); A6 "field edition" with answers compressed to keywords.
- **Margins:** generous — minimum 12 mm outer; the left margin is a functional game zone (see §8) and takes ~18 mm.
- **Paper intent:** uncoated, off-white, toothy enough for pencil (80–120 gsm munken/biotop feel). Never gloss. The design must assume graphite is the third ink.
- **Ink concept: two spot colors, risograph register.** 
  - **Ink 1 — Night:** a deep blue-black (letterpress ink, not pure black; think `#1c2733` as reference).
  - **Ink 2 — Lantern:** a burnt amber/ochre (reference `#a1712f`–`#b8834a`).
  - Optional third pass for special editions only: a muted **seaglass** green (reference `#4f7a6c`), reserved exclusively for the echo-mark margin.
- Slight, deliberate **misregistration** between the two inks (0.3–0.6 mm) is welcome — it should look hand-pressed, not broken. All essential strokes (lines to write on, boxes to strike) live in Night ink only, so the sheet still works photocopied in one color.

## 4. Typography (as visual material)

Three voices, kept strictly apart:

- **The traveler's voice** — a literary old-style serif with true italics (Cormorant Garamond or a cousin: Garamond, Caslon). Used for the road-name slot label, the five questions, facet names. The questions are set in *italic*, like marginalia.
- **The rules' voice** — a small, evenly spaced mono or grotesque with wide tracking, ALL-CAPS at tiny sizes (IBM Plex Mono or a typewriter face). Used for section labels, slot numbers, the oracle strip. This voice should feel *stamped or ticket-printed* — it may sit in the Lantern ink.
- **The player's voice** — no font. It is the pencil. Every answer area is ruled with a fine dotted or solid baseline in Night ink at ~40% tint, spaced for handwriting (7–8 mm line height).

No display lettering, no calligraphy, no distressed "aged" fonts. Age comes from the printing, never from the glyphs.

## 5. Mark language & illustration

Illustration is **sparse, linear, and single-weight** — fine pen/etching line, the density of a vintage field guide or a railway map, never filled or painted. Total illustrated area under ~15% of the page. Approved motif vocabulary (choose few):

- a small lantern (the game's emblem — one appearance only, ideally in the header)
- fog rendered as horizontal broken lines
- birds in silhouette, mid-distance
- a key, a ticket stub, a shell, an unsent letter
- topographic contour lines, dotted travel routes with a compass-less arrow
- postal ephemera: cancellation circles, a rubber-stamp frame, a faint "processed" mark (the Station's bureaucratic cosmology may appear only this subtly)
- stitching or string-and-pin lines connecting related zones

**Banned:** swords, shields, skulls, scrolls, celtic knots, wax seals, banners, coats of arms, dice illustrations, character portraits, borders that frame the whole page like a certificate.

## 6. The three facet emblems

Bone, Breath, and Dream each get a small emblem (≈8 mm), drawn in the same single-weight line, abstract enough to be redrawn by hand:

- **Bone** — something load-bearing: a bridge pile, a walking stick, a knuckle of stone.
- **Breath** — something in motion between people: a ripple, a spoken breath in cold air, wind through a doorway.
- **Dream** — something half-there: a keyhole with light behind it, a reflection breaking, a moth.

These emblems are the only place the design may be poetic-symbolic; everywhere else, clarity wins.

## 7. Composition

Vertical rhythm of a ruled journal page; zones separated by fine rules and breathing room, never boxes-within-boxes. Reading order top to bottom:

```
┌─────┬──────────────────────────────────────┐
│  e  │  tiny lantern · REVERIE · TRAVELER   │
│  c  │  Road-name  ________________________ │
│  h  │                                      │
│  o  │  the five questions, italic, each    │
│  ·  │  over ruled writing lines            │
│  m  │                                      │
│  a  │  BONE      BREATH      DREAM         │
│  r  │  emblem    emblem      emblem        │
│  k  │  ○○○ dots  ○○○         ○○○           │
│  s  │  ☐☐☐ weathering under each           │
│     │                                      │
│  ○  │  SATCHEL: seven drawn slots in a row │
│  ○  │  (slot 7 dashed, stamped "burden")   │
│  ○  │                                      │
│  ○  │  WAYMARKS: ruled lines w/ ◆ bullets  │
│  ○  │                                      │
│     │  oracle strip, stamped, footer       │
└─────┴──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Hierarchy: Road-name is the largest element by far. Facet emblems are the second visual anchor. Everything else is quiet.

## 8. The signature: the echo-mark margin

Spend the design's boldness here and nowhere else. The left margin is a true notebook margin, separated by one fine Lantern-ink vertical rule, holding **five to seven empty circles** (≈9 mm) running down the page. Players invent and draw a small symbol in a circle whenever a motif recurs in play — the margin becomes a private index of the story's obsessions.

Direction: the circles may be printed as faint cancellation-stamp rings (postal register), slightly irregular, in seaglass or Lantern at low tint. One circle may be printed *already half-faded*, as if a previous traveler's mark was erased. Nothing else on the sheet gets this kind of storytelling.

## 9. Field-by-field notes

- **Road-name:** one long writing line under a small italic prompt ("What are you called out here?"). Consider a faint luggage-tag or ticket outline behind the line — at most 10% tint, optional.
- **Five questions:** set as italic marginalia questions, each above 1–2 ruled lines. Two of them carry a tiny cross-reference in the rules' voice: *keepsake → slot 1*, *burden → slot 7*, and *dream-place → margin ○*. These cross-references are printed like catalogue notations.
- **Facets:** emblem, name in small caps serif, three-word gloss in the rules' voice, three empty **circles** (dots to be filled by pencil), three empty **squares** beneath (weathering, to be struck through). Circles and squares are hand-wobbled, not geometric-perfect.
- **Satchel:** seven slots drawn as a single row of open rectangles with hand-ruled edges, numbered in tiny stamp digits. Slot 7: dashed border, the word *burden* stamped diagonally at ~15% Lantern tint.
- **Waymarks:** 5–6 ruled lines with a small ◆ at each line's head. A one-line italic prompt above: *one line per passage — something you now know, carry, or can't un-see.*
- **Oracle strip:** the footer, in the rules' voice, printed as if rubber-stamped slightly askew: `1 NO,AND · 2 NO · 3 NO,BUT · 4 YES,BUT · 5 YES · 6 YES,AND`.

## 10. Texture & imperfection — the rules of wear

Real wear only, never simulated damage:
- Allowed: ink misregistration, uneven ink coverage/roller texture, paper tooth, a slightly bowed rule, stamp elements at a small angle.
- **Banned:** fake coffee stains, fake fold shadows, fake burnt edges, fake tape, drop shadows, any digital "grunge overlay" texture pack look.

The sheet earns its stains from its owner.

## 11. Deliverables

1. A5 sheet, two-ink version (Night + Lantern) — the canonical edition.
2. One-ink photocopy-safe version (Night only) — must remain fully functional.
3. A4 two-up with cut mark.
4. Optional: three-ink special edition (adds seaglass to the margin only).

## 12. Image-generation seed (if executing with an image model)

> A5 portrait character sheet for a quiet travel roleplaying game, styled as a page from a wanderer's field journal printed letterpress/risograph in two inks — deep blue-black and burnt amber — on toothy off-white paper. Fine single-weight etching-style line art only: a small lantern emblem in the header, faint fog lines, postal cancellation circles down a left margin rail. Literary old-style serif italic questions over ruled pencil lines; tiny stamped typewriter labels. Sections: large road-name line; five handwritten-answer questions; three facets (Bone, Breath, Dream) each with a small abstract emblem, three empty circles and three empty squares; a row of seven hand-ruled satchel slots, the seventh dashed and stamped "burden"; ruled waymark lines with small diamond bullets; a rubber-stamped oracle results strip as the footer. Slight ink misregistration, generous margins, no fantasy clichés, no borders, no distress overlays — a calm, liminal, ticket-office field document waiting for a pencil.

## 13. Acceptance checklist

- [ ] Works photocopied in one color
- [ ] A player can redraw the structure by hand in two minutes
- [ ] Nothing on it would look at home on a fantasy-RPG sheet
- [ ] Illustration under ~15% of page area; one lantern only
- [ ] All writing areas comfortably fit real handwriting (7–8 mm)
- [ ] The echo-mark margin is the single most distinctive element
- [ ] No simulated damage anywhere
