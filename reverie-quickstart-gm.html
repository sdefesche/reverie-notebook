Reverie Quickstart

RUNNING THE HUNT

a GM guide for a table raised on D&D · 60–120 minutes
The Reframe

What you're actually running

Same rules as full Reverie, different emphasis. You're running a monster hunt with a puzzle core: the mystery's fact tally is the dungeon, the Telling is the boss key, the showdown is the boss fight. Park the dreamy stuff — no dream passages, no journal rituals, no talk about what places feel like unless it lands as creepy. Creepy always lands.

The pitch to say out loud before you start: "This monster can't be beaten by rolling damage. It can be beaten by anyone who figures it out. Every question you ask is an attack."

Structure

The 90-minute clock

0–15Setup. Hand out pregens (in the adventure) or build hunters — 6 points, name, one thing you carry, one thing you fear. Read the pitch. Open on the hook scene.
15–60The investigation. Three locations, each holding clues and one risky moment. Facts accumulate on a visible tally — write it where they can see it. The monster's threat track ticks in parallel, also visible. The race IS the tension.
60–65The Telling. Seven facts (or when time demands — use the valve: "you know enough, tell me what it is"). Let them build the weakness. Say yes to the coolest coherent version.
65–85The showdown. Their plan, its terms. Plans that use the weakness work or roll two extra dice. Plans that ignore it hurt.
85–90Loot and residue. The Vessel, one open question for next time, done.
The Dials

Tuning for this table

  • Roll loud and often. D&D kids like dice in hands. Call Crossings freely for physical stuff — chases, climbs, grabs. Let them roll the Oracle themselves; interpreting a "yes, but…" out loud is the game teaching itself.
  • Both tracks stay visible. Facts and threat marks on paper in the middle of the table. When the monster gains a mark, mark it slowly, while looking at them. Dread is a resource.
  • Feed the tally generously. Any smart question, searched location, or interrogated villager can yield a fact. Aim for a fact every 5–6 minutes. If they stall, a Figure volunteers something — attached to a price.
  • Tolls are mischief, not homework. On a tie, pick fun trouble: the lantern goes out, something learns their name, the rope stays behind. And run the look-up: ten seconds, real room, first thing noticed enters the game. Kids treat this as a superpower. It is.
  • Damage is real, death isn't. Mark weathering without mercy — but "out of the hunt" means dramatically carried off, cheering from the bench, guaranteed back next time. Stakes without tears.
  • The two-answer secret. You get two direct answers per mystery (you posed it). Spend them like potions — one to rescue a stalled tally, one held for the end. Everything else: the Oracle decides, in front of everyone. Kids trust a die they watched land.
The Net

The default truth

Full Reverie assembles the monster's truth at the table with no answer fixed in advance. Keep that goal — the Telling is theirs — but for a first hunt, carry a default truth in your pocket (the adventure provides one): a solution that fits most facts they're likely to gather. If their Telling coheres, theirs wins, always — even if it's not yours. The default is only for rescue: a stalled table, a bedtime deadline. A table that beats the monster with a weakness they invented will talk about it for weeks. That's the product.

Table Voice

Say it like this

Phrases that do work

"That's a fact. Write it down." (reward)
"The Hound just gained a mark." (clock)
"What do you do — not what do you roll?" (agency)
"Tie! Everybody look up. Ten seconds." (the room joins)
"You COULD fight it now. You don't know what it is yet. Your call." (the lesson)

One safety line, said once, lightly: anyone can pause or steer away from anything, no reason needed — the fear on the sheet belongs to the hunter, not the kid holding the pencil. Then go.

Afterwards

The door back to full Reverie

End every hunt with one unanswered question written down — "the coin in the Hound's collar: whose was it?" That's a residue, and it's your bridge: next hunt opens from it, and without announcing anything you're now playing with echoes. The dreamier machinery can arrive the same way, one piece at a time, whenever this table starts asking the questions that need it. They will.
