Lineage

No game begins from nothing, and this one is glad of its ancestors. Reverie is built in the posture of Cairn (Yochai Gal, itself CC BY-SA) and its parent Into the Odd (Chris McDowall): the slot inventory, the fast questionnaire of a character, fiction-first rulings, advancement by living rather than leveling, and the conviction that danger is best avoided. Its transplanted heart comes from elsewhere: the graded oracle stands in the solo journaling tradition that runs from Mythic GM Emulator through Ironsworn's descendants; the Riddle is Black Stories with the answer removed, so the table builds the truth it deduces; Perils owe a debt to the progress clocks of Blades in the Dark (John Harper).

The organs with no ancestor we know of — the Threshold Moment, weathering written as biography, Tolls collected by the echo of a dog-eared page, and the notebook as the game's memory engine — are offered onward under the same terms, for whoever needs them next.

No text was taken from any of these works; what was learned from them is gratefully acknowledged.
