Quick Reference · Keeper of the Lantern

REVERIE

you hold the lantern, not the answers

Your whole preparation — three things, no more

an opening image · one fragment · one tension

You are facilitator and surprise-bearer, never opponent or novelist. You play a traveler too — they simply stand quieter this passage. You don't know the truth of any riddle; a riddle's poser (if invented, not rolled) may answer at most two questions directly across its life — usually you, for your prepared fragment. Rolled riddles are Oracle-only.

The passage — six beats

  1. Threshold — one breath of silence, then the image.
  2. Wander — travel, talk, small truths.
  3. Fragment — when energy dips, drop it (or roll d66).
  4. Crossing — ensure one risk that matters.
  5. Echo — the session's echo must land by now.
  6. Residue — each names one image; write & mark them.

Session start — roll the echo

1–2 last session's pages
3–4 any dog-eared page, eyes closed
5–6 oldest unresolved mark

Pick one marked thing. It returns this session, transformed: same motif, different vessel. Don't force it early.

When to call for what

  • Uncertain + costly → Crossing. Otherwise it just happens.
  • Nobody knows → Oracle — including you. Ask it openly.
  • Unexplained → open a riddle page, start the fact tally.
  • Tie → Threshold Moment: succeed + Toll (aim it at a Peril if apt) + ten seconds, eyes up, world in.
  • Turned aside in danger → 1 box; 2 if the World die won by 3+.
  • Riddle past 15 questions → valve: truth told with what stands.

Keeper's habits

  • Describe what places feel like before what's in them.
  • Answer questions with details, not conclusions.
  • Hand authorship away: "what rumor explains this?"
  • Let contradictions stand; some mysteries stay at six facts.
  • Danger is real — telegraph it, honor retreat.
  • End on resonance. Never wrap up.

Perils — patient dangers

name it aloud · three boxes
advances on: failed Crossing vs it · a Toll fed to it · no,and about safety
three marks → it arrives — frame the unavoidable scene

Every Peril is a riddle: seven facts reveal how it ends. Two active at most. Urgent perils feed on every Toll — they arrive in ~4 scenes instead of ~6.
